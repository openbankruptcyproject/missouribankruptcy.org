Who Administers Missouri Bankruptcy Cases

Every bankruptcy case has a trustee — a private party (not a federal employee) appointed to administer the case. What the trustee does depends on the chapter, and who it is depends on the district.

What Trustees Do

  • Chapter 7 trustees liquidate non-exempt property and distribute proceeds to creditors. They chair the § 341 meeting of creditors, review schedules for accuracy, and investigate potential asset recoveries.
  • Chapter 13 and Subchapter V trustees don't typically liquidate — they collect your plan payments, distribute them to creditors according to the confirmed plan, chair the § 341 meeting, and make recommendations to the court on plan confirmation and modifications.

All trustees answer to the United States Trustee Program — the Justice Department component that oversees the federal bankruptcy system.

Neither trustee is your attorney. They represent the bankruptcy estate and the creditor body, not you. But they're neutral: their job is to enforce the statute, not to advance any party's interest.

The U.S. Trustee is not the trustee assigned to your individual case. They oversee the panel trustees, investigate potential abuse under § 707(b), and can object to plan confirmations. If you believe your panel trustee is acting improperly, the U.S. Trustee's office is where to escalate.

Chapter 7 Panel Trustees

Chapter 7 trustees rotate from a panel maintained by the U.S. Trustee for each district. When you file Chapter 7, you are assigned a specific panel trustee — you do not choose.

Eastern District of Missouri

The E.D. Mo. clerk maintains an official panel-trustee contact list: Panel_Trustee_Contact_List.pdf (last revised October 14, 2025). Notable panel members include David A. Sosne, a long-tenured St. Louis panel trustee. Consult the PDF for the current complete roster.

Western District of Missouri

The W.D. Mo. Chapter 7 panel roster is maintained by the U.S. Trustee Region 13 office and the W.D. Mo. clerk. Contact the clerk's office or UST Region 13 for the current roster.

The trustee assigned to your case will appear on the § 341 meeting notice the court sends within a week or two of filing. You can also pull the assignment from the PACER docket under "Meeting of Creditors."

Subchapter V Trustees (Small-Business Ch. 11)

Subchapter V of Chapter 11 — the small-business reorganization tool created by the Small Business Reorganization Act of 2019 — uses case-specific trustees rather than a standing trustee. The U.S. Trustee appoints a Sub V trustee when the case is filed.

These trustees have a hybrid role: they facilitate plan negotiation between debtor and creditors (more mediator-like than a Chapter 7 or 13 trustee) and supervise plan performance.

Sub V trustee appointments in Missouri come from a regional Sub V trustee pool maintained by UST Region 13. The trustee's name will appear in the opening docket entries of the case and on the § 341 notice.

How to Find the Trustee on Your Case

Three ways:

  1. § 341 notice. Sent to you by the court after filing; identifies the trustee assigned and the meeting date, time, and location.
  2. PACER docket sheet. The "Trustee" field lists the current trustee; re-check if trustees rotate mid-case.
  3. Clerk's office. Can look up trustee assignment by case number over the phone.

For Chapter 13: plan payments always go to the Standing Trustee for your district (Fink in W.D. Mo.; Daugherty in E.D. Mo.), regardless of what the § 341 notice shows for individual proceedings.

What does a bankruptcy trustee do?

Chapter 7 trustees liquidate non-exempt property and distribute proceeds to creditors. They chair the § 341 meeting, review schedules, and investigate potential asset recoveries. Chapter 13 and Subchapter V trustees collect plan payments, distribute them to creditors, chair the § 341 meeting, and make recommendations on plan confirmation. All trustees are private parties appointed by the U.S. Trustee Program, not federal employees.
