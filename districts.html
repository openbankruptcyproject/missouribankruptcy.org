Where Missouri Bankruptcy Cases Are Filed

Missouri has two federal bankruptcy courts, not one. Which court you file in depends on where you live. File in the wrong district and the case gets transferred or dismissed. Get the district right from the start.

Two Districts, Seven Divisional Courthouses

Western District of Missouri (W.D. Mo.)

  • Bankruptcy Judges: Chief Judge Cynthia A. Norton, Judge Brian T. Fenimore
  • Divisional offices: Kansas City · Jefferson City · St. Joseph · Springfield
  • Coverage: Western and central Missouri counties, including the Kansas City metro, central Missouri, the Ozarks, and the northwest
  • Electronic filing: CM/ECF at ecf.mowb.uscourts.gov
  • Court website: mow.uscourts.gov/bankruptcy
  • Local rules: mow.uscourts.gov/bankruptcy/rules

Eastern District of Missouri (E.D. Mo.)

  • Bankruptcy Judges: Chief Judge Bonnie L. Clair, Judge Kathy A. Surratt-States, Judge Brian C. Walsh
  • Clerk of Court: Dana C. McWay · (314) 244-4500
  • Divisional offices: St. Louis · Cape Girardeau · Hannibal
  • Coverage: Eastern Missouri counties, including the St. Louis metro, southeast Missouri, and northeast Missouri along the Mississippi
  • Court website: moeb.uscourts.gov
  • Pro se tools: eSR (Electronic Self-Representation) + EDSS (Electronic Document Submission System)

Finding your exact county assignment: For the authoritative county-to-division mapping, consult the divisional pages on each court's website. Filing in the wrong division is a common pro se error; call the clerk's office if there's any doubt before filing.

W.D. Mo. - Divisional Details

Kansas City is the volume division of the Western District. The court runs a regular Chapter 13 motion docket where routine motions (plan modifications, hardship discharges, plan-payment defaults) are called in batches. Chapter 13 plan payments for all W.D. Mo. cases route to Richard V. Fink's office (the standing trustee), regardless of which division your case is in.

Jefferson City serves central Missouri counties along the Missouri River corridor - a rural/small-city mix centered on Cole County. St. Joseph covers the far-northwest counties including Buchanan. Springfield covers the southwestern counties, including the Ozarks - Greene, Christian, Taney, Webster, and surrounding areas.

For a pro se filer, the division you file in is determined by the county you reside in or have your principal place of business in. Routine filings happen electronically regardless of division; hearings are held at the division seat.

E.D. Mo. - Divisional Details

Eastern Division (St. Louis) sits at the Thomas F. Eagleton U.S. Courthouse, 111 South 10th Street, 4th Floor, St. Louis, MO 63102. It hears the largest share of E.D. Mo. cases.

Southeastern Division (Cape Girardeau) is at the Rush Hudson Limbaugh Sr. U.S. Courthouse, 555 Independence Street, Cape Girardeau, MO 63703 - handles southeast Missouri "Bootheel" counties.

Northern Division (Hannibal) is at the Federal Building, 801 Broadway, Hannibal, MO 63401 - handles the northeast counties along the Mississippi.

The E.D. Mo. clerk's office maintains a panel trustee contact list (Panel_Trustee_Contact_List.pdf, last revised October 14, 2025) and specific hearing-procedure guidance. Signature requirements for e-filed documents were updated in January 2024.

Chapter 13 plan payments for all E.D. Mo. cases go to Diana S. Daugherty's office (the standing trustee), regardless of which division your case is in.

Which District Covers Me? The Venue Rule

The rule that controls is 28 U.S.C. § 1408: you file in the district where you have lived (or maintained your principal place of business) for the greater part of the 180 days before filing.

  • If you've lived in the same Missouri county for more than 90 days before filing, that county's district controls.
  • If you moved to Missouri recently, you may need to file in the district you moved from - not the one you currently live in.
  • If you cross the state line for work or have property in another state, consult an attorney before filing.

Filing in the wrong venue results in transfer, dismissal, or forced re-filing - at best a delay, at worst the loss of filing fees. Get the venue right the first time.

Electronic Filing for Pro Se Debtors

W.D. Mo. - CM/ECF

Use ecf.mowb.uscourts.gov. Pro se filers can request a filer ID through the clerk's office. Credit card filing-fee payment is handled through pay.gov.

E.D. Mo. - eSR and EDSS

  • eSR (Electronic Self-Representation) - for starting a new voluntary petition online.
  • EDSS (Electronic Document Submission System) - for uploading filings after a case is open.

Both districts accept in-person filings at the clerk's windows during business hours. Each clerk's office also maintains a drop box for after-hours filings.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many federal bankruptcy courts does Missouri have?

Two. The U.S. Bankruptcy Court for the Western District of Missouri (W.D. Mo.) and the U.S. Bankruptcy Court for the Eastern District of Missouri (E.D. Mo.). Which court you file in depends on where you have lived (or maintained your principal place of business) for the greater part of the 180 days before filing, per 28 U.S.C. § 1408.

Who are the current W.D. Mo. bankruptcy judges?

Chief Judge Cynthia A. Norton and Judge Brian T. Fenimore. The court has divisional offices in Kansas City, Jefferson City, St. Joseph, and Springfield.

Who are the current E.D. Mo. bankruptcy judges?

Chief Judge Bonnie L. Clair, Judge Kathy A. Surratt-States, and Judge Brian C. Walsh. The clerk of court is Dana C. McWay. The primary courthouse is the Thomas F. Eagleton U.S. Courthouse at 111 South 10th Street, 4th Floor, St. Louis, MO 63102.

Can I file bankruptcy online in Missouri?

Yes. Both Missouri bankruptcy districts support electronic filing. W.D. Mo. uses CM/ECF at ecf.mowb.uscourts.gov; pro se filers can request a filer ID from the clerk. E.D. Mo. offers two dedicated pro se tools: eSR (Electronic Self-Representation) for starting a new voluntary petition online, and EDSS (Electronic Document Submission System) for filings in an existing case.
