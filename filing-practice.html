What Filing Bankruptcy in Missouri Actually Looks Like

Statutes and rules describe the rules on paper. What happens in a Missouri bankruptcy case also depends on local court practice — which varies by division, sometimes by judge, and has shifted significantly since 2020.

Kansas City Metro (W.D. Mo.) — The Volume Division

The Kansas City division of the U.S. Bankruptcy Court for the Western District of Missouri hears the largest share of W.D. Mo. cases. The court runs a regular Chapter 13 motion docket where routine motions (plan modifications, hardship discharges, plan-payment defaults) are called in batches.

Chapter 13 plan payments statewide in W.D. Mo. route to Richard V. Fink's office (the standing trustee) regardless of which division your case is in.

Jefferson City, St. Joseph, and Springfield are the other three W.D. Mo. divisions. Jefferson City covers central counties along the Missouri River corridor. St. Joseph handles the northwest counties including Buchanan. Springfield covers the southwest including Greene, Christian, and Taney Counties — the Ozarks. See Missouri federal districts for the full breakdown.

St. Louis Metro (E.D. Mo.) — The Eastern Division

The E.D. Mo. Eastern Division sits at the Thomas F. Eagleton U.S. Courthouse in downtown St. Louis. The Southeastern Division sits in Cape Girardeau, and the Northern Division in Hannibal. The St. Louis division hears the largest share of E.D. Mo. cases; Cape Girardeau and Hannibal handle regional rural filings and specific Southeast/Northeast county assignments.

E.D. Mo. updated signature requirements for electronic filings in January 2024 and has established video-conference procedures for certain hearings. Confirm current hearing modality against your notice.

All E.D. Mo. Chapter 13 plan payments go to Diana S. Daugherty's office (the standing trustee). See trustees page for full details.

Section 341 Meeting of Creditors — Video vs. In-Person

Both Missouri bankruptcy districts moved § 341 meetings to video (Zoom / telephone) during the 2020-2022 period. As of 2026, some meetings remain video and others have reverted to in-person depending on case type, division, and trustee.

How to know for sure: your § 341 meeting notice from the court tells you exactly where and how your meeting will be conducted. If the notice lists a specific physical courthouse address, it is in-person. If it lists a Zoom link or call-in number, it is video or telephonic.

Show up on time whether in-person or video. Missing your § 341 is grounds for dismissal.

Filing Fee Waivers (IFP) — Chapter 7 Only

Under 28 U.S.C. § 1930(f) and the accompanying Judicial Conference guidelines, a Chapter 7 debtor whose household income is below 150% of the federal poverty guideline for the debtor's household size may apply for a filing fee waiver using Official Form 103B.

The waiver is discretionary — the court can deny it based on:

  • Ability to pay in installments
  • Recent gift transfers or asset dispositions
  • Other factors suggesting the filer can afford the fee

No IFP waiver for Chapter 13. Chapter 13 filers must pay the filing fee or qualify for an installment plan. If you are below 150% FPL and considering bankruptcy, Chapter 7 has a cost advantage you may not have in Chapter 13.

If the waiver is granted, the entire Chapter 7 filing fee is eliminated. If denied, installment payments over up to 120 days are the standard alternative.

Pro Se Electronic Filing — What to Do First

W.D. Mo. — CM/ECF

Use ecf.mowb.uscourts.gov. Pro se filers can request a filer ID from the clerk's office. Filing fee payment is handled through pay.gov.

E.D. Mo. — eSR and EDSS

  • eSR (Electronic Self-Representation) — for starting a new voluntary petition online.
  • EDSS (Electronic Document Submission System) — for uploading filings after a case is open.

Both districts accept in-person filings at the clerk's windows during business hours, and both maintain drop boxes for after-hours filings. If your case is time-sensitive (e.g., eve of a garnishment or foreclosure), the in-person drop box is your backstop when electronic filing fails.

Subchapter V (Small Business Chapter 11) in Missouri

Sub V cases in Missouri are assigned a case-specific trustee appointed from the UST Region 13 Sub V trustee pool. The trustee functions as a mediator between debtor and creditors, more than a Chapter 7 liquidator — the role is to help reach a confirmable reorganization plan within the expedited Sub V timeline.

Key Sub V Deadlines

  • Status conference: held not later than 60 days after order for relief (11 U.S.C. § 1188)
  • Plan filing: debtor must file a plan not later than 90 days after order for relief, absent extension for circumstances beyond debtor's control (11 U.S.C. § 1189)

Sub V cases in the 8th Circuit grew about 275% in Q1 2024 vs. Q1 2023 (Thompson Coburn, "Subchapter V Practice in the Eighth Circuit: The First Four Years" (May 2024)). Small-business debtors considering Sub V should understand that the expedited timeline means decisions about operating cash, secured debt treatment, and plan economics must be made quickly — often before counsel has full visibility into the case.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my Section 341 meeting in person or by video in Missouri?

It depends. Both Missouri bankruptcy districts moved § 341 meetings to video (Zoom or telephone) during the 2020-2022 period. As of 2026, some meetings remain video and others have reverted to in-person depending on case type, division, and trustee. Your § 341 meeting notice from the court tells you exactly where and how: if the notice lists a specific physical courthouse address, it is in-person; if it lists a Zoom link or call-in number, it is video or telephonic.

Can I get a Chapter 7 filing fee waiver in Missouri?

Possibly. Under 28 U.S.C. § 1930(f) and Judicial Conference guidelines, a Chapter 7 debtor whose household income is below 150% of the federal poverty guideline for the debtor's household size may apply for a filing fee waiver using Official Form 103B. The waiver is discretionary. There is no IFP waiver for Chapter 13 — Chapter 13 filers must pay the filing fee or qualify for an installment plan.

How do I file bankruptcy electronically as a pro se debtor in Missouri?

W.D. Mo. uses CM/ECF at ecf.mowb.uscourts.gov; pro se filers can request a filer ID from the clerk. Filing fees are paid through pay.gov. E.D. Mo. offers two dedicated pro se tools: eSR (Electronic Self-Representation) for starting a new voluntary petition online, and EDSS (Electronic Document Submission System) for filings in an existing case. Both districts also accept in-person filings and have drop boxes for after-hours filings.

What is Subchapter V bankruptcy and how is it different?

Subchapter V of Chapter 11 is an expedited form of Chapter 11 for qualifying small businesses (created by the Small Business Reorganization Act of 2019). A case-specific trustee is appointed from the UST Region 13 Sub V trustee pool; the trustee facilitates plan negotiation rather than liquidating. Key deadlines: status conference 60 days from filing (11 U.S.C. § 1188); plan filing within 90 days absent extension (11 U.S.C. § 1189). Short timelines mean strategic decisions happen fast.
