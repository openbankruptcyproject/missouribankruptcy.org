Free and Low-Cost Legal Help in Missouri

Three legal-aid organizations cover Missouri between them, plus the Missouri Bar's paid referral service and law-school clinics. Here is who actually helps with what - and what bankruptcy-specific help each can realistically offer.

Honest framing up front: most Missouri legal aid programs do NOT directly represent clients in Chapter 7 or Chapter 13 bankruptcy filings. They handle the consumer-law consequences of debt (eviction, garnishment defense, FDCPA claims, benefit-program issues) but typically don't file bankruptcies for low-income clients the way they file landlord-tenant actions. That gap matters - plan accordingly. Always verify current scope at intake.

The Missouri Bar Lawyer Referral Service

If you do not qualify for legal aid (income too high) but cannot afford full-price private representation, the Missouri Bar Lawyer Referral Service (LRS) offers low-cost referrals.

  • Fee: $50 for a consultation of up to 30 minutes with a Missouri-licensed attorney in your practice area
  • Phone: (573) 636-3635
  • Hours: Monday-Friday, 9:00 AM - 3:00 PM
  • Parent organization: The Missouri Bar (mobar.org) - go to "Find a Lawyer" for the official LRS page
  • Coverage: statewide; bankruptcy lawyers available in the referral network

What the LRS is: a paid referral to a lawyer who has registered with the Missouri Bar for a 30-minute consultation. What it is not: legal representation, a free service, or a guarantee that the lawyer you are referred to will take your case afterward.

Law School Clinics in Missouri

Missouri has multiple law schools, several with consumer-law or bankruptcy clinics that represent low-income clients as part of their clinical education programs. Clinic availability varies by academic year, funding, and faculty rotation.

  • UMKC School of Law (Kansas City)
  • University of Missouri School of Law (Columbia)
  • Saint Louis University School of Law (St. Louis)
  • Washington University School of Law (St. Louis)

Some may not have active bankruptcy clinics; others may have broader consumer-law clinics that occasionally take bankruptcy referrals. If you are in or near Kansas City, St. Louis, or Columbia, calling the local law school clinical office directly is the fastest way to learn what is currently available.

U.S. Trustee Pro Se Assistance

Both bankruptcy districts in Missouri provide pro-se procedural guidance through the clerk's office, and the U.S. Trustee for Region 13 maintains educational materials at justice.gov/ust/ust-regions-r13.

The UST does not provide legal advice or representation. They can explain procedure, direct you to approved credit-counseling and debtor-education providers, and clarify filing mechanics - but they cannot tell you whether bankruptcy is right for you or advise on strategy.

Income Eligibility Notes

Legal aid income eligibility is typically tied to the federal poverty level (commonly 125% to 200% of FPL depending on the program, matter type, and funding source). Thresholds update annually with the HHS poverty guidelines.

If you are near the threshold, apply anyway: some programs have priority intake categories (domestic violence survivors, elderly clients, veterans) or hardship exceptions that can qualify you above the standard cutoff. Call the intake office and ask.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Missouri legal aid handle bankruptcy cases?

Generally no, though it varies. The two main Missouri legal aid organizations (Legal Aid of Western Missouri and Legal Services of Eastern Missouri) primarily handle housing, consumer-law, family law, immigration, and public benefits matters. Bankruptcy representation is not listed as a primary practice area at either. They may help with downstream consequences of debt (eviction defense, garnishment defense) but will not typically file a Chapter 7 or Chapter 13 petition for a client. Verify current scope at intake.

What is the Missouri Bar Lawyer Referral Service fee?

The Missouri Bar Lawyer Referral Service charges $50 for a referral, which entitles you to a consultation of up to 30 minutes with a Missouri-licensed attorney in your practice area. Phone: (573) 636-3635. Hours: Monday-Friday, 9:00 AM to 3:00 PM. Bankruptcy lawyers are available in the referral network.

Are there law school bankruptcy clinics in Missouri?

Missouri has multiple law schools (UMKC, University of Missouri, Saint Louis University, Washington University), several with consumer-law or bankruptcy clinics that take low-income clients. Clinic availability varies by academic year and funding. Calling the local law school clinical office directly is the fastest way to learn what is currently available.
