The 341 Meeting of Creditors in Kansas City

The 341 meeting of creditors is a brief hearing about 30 days after filing where the trustee asks questions under oath. Most last 5-10 minutes.

What Is the 341 Meeting?

The 341 meeting (named after 11 U.S.C. section 341) is a required hearing in every bankruptcy case. The trustee assigned to your case asks you questions under oath about your finances, assets, income, and the information in your petition.

Despite its formal name, the 341 meeting is not held in front of a judge. Most meetings in Kansas City last only 5-10 minutes.

Kansas City 341 Meeting Location

W.D. Mo. -- Kansas City Division: 400 E. 9th St, Kansas City, MO 64106

Many 341 meetings are now conducted by phone or video conference. Your attorney will confirm the format.

What to Bring

  • Government-issued photo ID (driver's license or passport)
  • Proof of Social Security number (SS card, W-2, or 1099)
  • Most recent tax return
  • Recent pay stubs (last 60 days)
  • Bank statements
