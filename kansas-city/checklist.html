Kansas City Bankruptcy Filing Checklist

A step-by-step checklist for Kansas City residents preparing to file bankruptcy. Covers documents, credit counseling, and filing requirements.

Documents You Will Need

  • Tax returns -- last 2 years
  • Pay stubs -- last 6 months
  • Bank statements -- last 6 months
  • Mortgage statements or lease agreement
  • Vehicle titles and loan statements
  • Credit card and loan statements
  • Medical bills
  • Court judgments and garnishment orders
  • Insurance policies
  • Retirement account statements

Steps Before Filing

  1. Complete credit counseling -- from an approved agency ($15-25). Required within 180 days before filing.
  2. Gather all documents -- see list above.
  3. List all debts -- every creditor, amount, and account number.
  4. List all assets -- everything you own and its approximate value.
  5. Review exemptions -- determine what is protected under Missouri exemptions.
  6. Choose Chapter 7 or 13 -- based on income, assets, and goals.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Do not transfer property before filing -- this can be reversed and may constitute fraud
  • Do not pay back family members -- preferential transfers can be clawed back by the trustee
  • Do not run up new debt -- charges made shortly before filing may not be dischargeable
  • List all debts and assets -- failure to disclose is perjury and can result in denial of discharge
  • Complete both courses -- credit counseling before filing and debtor education before discharge

Frequently Asked Questions

What documents do I need for bankruptcy?

Tax returns (last 2 years), pay stubs (last 6 months), bank statements, vehicle titles, mortgage statements, credit card statements, medical bills, and a list of all debts and assets.

Do I need credit counseling before filing?

Yes. You must complete a credit counseling course from an approved agency within 180 days before filing. Cost is typically $15-25.

What are common mistakes?

Transferring property before filing, paying back family members, running up new debt, failing to list all debts and assets, and not completing required courses.
