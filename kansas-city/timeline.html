Kansas City Bankruptcy Timeline - What to Expect

A detailed timeline of the bankruptcy process in Kansas City. From preparation through discharge, here is what to expect at each stage.

Chapter 7 Timeline

  1. Weeks 1-4: Consultation and Preparation - Meet with an attorney. Gather documents. Complete credit counseling ($15-25).
  2. Day 1: Filing - Petition filed. Automatic stay takes effect immediately. All collection stops.
  3. Day 30-45: 341 Meeting - Brief meeting of creditors at 400 E. 9th St, Kansas City. Usually 5-10 minutes.
  4. Day 30-60: Debtor Education - Complete the second required financial course ($10-25).
  5. Day 90-120: Discharge - Court enters discharge order. Case closed shortly after.

Chapter 13 Timeline

  1. Before filing: Credit counseling, document gathering, plan preparation.
  2. Day 1: Petition filed. Automatic stay takes effect.
  3. Day 14: Proposed plan filed.
  4. Day 30: First plan payment to trustee.
  5. Day 30-45: 341 meeting of creditors.
  6. Day 45-120: Confirmation hearing.
  7. Years 1-5: Monthly payments. Report income changes.
  8. Plan completion: Debtor education course. Discharge entered.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does Chapter 7 take?

From filing to discharge, Chapter 7 typically takes 3-4 months. The 341 meeting occurs about 30 days after filing.

How long does Chapter 13 take?

Chapter 13 requires completing a 3-5 year repayment plan. Plan confirmation usually happens 45-120 days after filing.

When does the automatic stay take effect?

The automatic stay takes effect the instant your petition is filed with the court - not when creditors find out.
