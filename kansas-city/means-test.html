Kansas City Means Test: Do You Qualify for Chapter 7?

The means test determines whether you can file Chapter 7 bankruptcy. It compares your income to the Missouri state median for your household size.

Step-by-Step Walkthrough

  1. Calculate your current monthly income (CMI) - Add up all income from the past 6 full calendar months. Divide by 6 for monthly, multiply by 12 for annual.
  2. Determine household size - Count everyone who depends on your income for support.
  3. Compare to median - If at or below the median, you pass and qualify for Chapter 7.
  4. If above: Calculate deductions - Subtract IRS-approved expenses, secured debt payments, and priority debts.
  5. Calculate disposable income - Multiply remaining monthly disposable income by 60. If less than $9,075, you pass.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the means test?

The means test compares your income to Missouri median income. If below, you qualify for Chapter 7 automatically.

What is the Missouri median income for 2026?

Single filer: $56,847. 2-person: $72,036. 3-person: $85,023. 4-person: $104,670.

What if I fail the means test?

You can file Chapter 13 instead, wait until income drops, or challenge with special circumstances.
