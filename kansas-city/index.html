Chapter 7 vs. Chapter 13: Which Is Right for You?

Chapter 7 -- Fresh Start

Chapter 7 eliminates most unsecured debts (credit cards, medical bills, personal loans) in about 3-4 months. You must pass a means test based on your income.

  • Discharge in 3-4 months
  • Eliminates credit cards, medical debt
  • Must pass the means test
  • May lose non-exempt property
  • Filing fee: $338
Learn more about Chapter 7 →

Chapter 13 -- Payment Plan

Chapter 13 lets you keep your property while repaying debts over 3-5 years. Good for saving a home from foreclosure or a car from repossession.

  • 3-5 year repayment plan
  • Keep your home and car
  • Catch up on missed payments
  • No means test requirement
  • Filing fee: $313

How Much Does Bankruptcy Cost in Kansas City?

  • Chapter 7 filing fee: $338 (can request installment payments)
  • Chapter 13 filing fee: $313 (can be paid through your plan)
  • Attorney fees: Typically $1,000-$2,000 for Chapter 7; $2,500-$4,000 for Chapter 13
  • Credit counseling: $15-25 per course (two required)

Fee waivers are available for Chapter 7 filers whose income is below 150% of the federal poverty guidelines.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does bankruptcy cost in Kansas City?

Chapter 7 filing fees are $338 and Chapter 13 filing fees are $313. Attorney fees typically range from $1,000-$2,000 for Chapter 7 and $2,500-$4,000 for Chapter 13. Full cost breakdown.

Will I lose my house if I file bankruptcy?

Missouri's homestead exemption protects up to $15,000 of equity. In Chapter 13, you can keep your home while catching up on missed payments. Learn more.

Where do I file for bankruptcy in Kansas City?

Missouri-side residents file in the Western District of Missouri (400 E. 9th St, Kansas City, MO 64106). Kansas-side residents file in the District of Kansas (500 State Ave, Kansas City, KS 66101).

How long does bankruptcy take?

Chapter 7 typically takes 3-4 months. Chapter 13 requires completing a 3-5 year repayment plan. The 341 meeting of creditors happens about 30 days after filing.
