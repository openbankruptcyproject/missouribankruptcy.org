Stop Wage Garnishment in St. Louis

Bankruptcy stops wage garnishment immediately. The automatic stay takes effect the instant your petition is filed.

Automatic Stay Stops Garnishment

Under 11 U.S.C. section 362, all wage garnishment must stop the moment you file. Your attorney notifies your employer and the creditor.

Missouri garnishment limit: lesser of 25% of disposable earnings or amount exceeding 30x federal minimum wage ($217.50/week).

How does bankruptcy stop garnishment?

The automatic stay under section 362 immediately prohibits all wage garnishment for pre-bankruptcy debts.

Stopping Wage Garnishment in St. Louis - How Bankruptcy Works

If a creditor is already garnishing your wages in St. Louis, bankruptcy can stop it the same day you file. The automatic stay under 11 U.S.C. section 362 is the most powerful injunction in American law - it binds every creditor the moment your petition hits the clerk's docket, even before the judge has reviewed your schedules. For garnishment specifically, the stay halts any further deductions from your paycheck and, in many cases, can recover garnishment payments taken in the 90 days before you filed.

A typical garnishment starts with a lawsuit. The creditor sues, gets a default judgment (most consumer cases are defaults), and then obtains a writ of garnishment served on your employer. Under federal law, creditors can take up to 25 percent of your disposable earnings or the amount by which your weekly earnings exceed 30 times the federal minimum wage, whichever is less. Missouri law may provide additional protections or slightly different calculations. Child support and tax garnishments have their own rules and can take more.

When you file bankruptcy, your attorney (or you, if pro se) immediately notifies your employer and the garnishing creditor that the stay is in effect. Most employers stop withholding on the next pay cycle. If the employer continues garnishing after notice, they become liable for the improper deduction and you can recover it. The creditor that initiated the garnishment can face sanctions and actual damages for stay violations under 11 U.S.C. section 362(k).

Recovering Pre-Filing Garnishment Payments

Under 11 U.S.C. section 547, you may be able to claw back garnishment payments made in the 90 days before filing as "preferential transfers." If the creditor received more than $600 during that window, the trustee can demand the money back, or you can pursue it directly as an exemption avoidance action under section 522(h). This recovery is not automatic - someone has to file the avoidance action - but it is a real tool for recovering thousands of dollars that were garnished before you filed.

Frequently Asked Questions

How fast does garnishment stop after I file bankruptcy in St. Louis?

The automatic stay is effective the moment your petition is filed. In practice, actual withholding stops on the next pay cycle after your employer is notified. Your attorney should fax or email a copy of the filed petition to your employer's payroll department the same day you file.

Can my employer fire me for filing bankruptcy?

No. 11 U.S.C. section 525 prohibits private employers from discharging an employee solely because they filed bankruptcy. Government employers are also prohibited from discriminating in hiring or employment. If your employer retaliates, you have a claim for damages and reinstatement.

Will child support garnishments stop in a St. Louis bankruptcy?

No. 11 U.S.C. section 362(b)(2) exempts domestic support obligations from the automatic stay. Child support and alimony garnishments continue unimpeded through bankruptcy. These debts are also non-dischargeable under section 523(a)(5). If child support arrearage is your problem, Chapter 13 is usually the right tool - it lets you cure the arrearage over the plan term while the current payments continue.
